## The failure mode: adverse selection, cleanly quantified

This is *not* the "fill% too low" branch and not the "hit-rate-bound" branch from the failure guide — fill rate is healthy at ~62%. It's the toxic-fill branch, and the `missed`/`hit_m` diagnostic nails it:

- **up6 @ 0.1%:** the 26.5% trigger-time hit rate splits into **13.2% among filled** vs **47.8% among unfilled** (whose gross endpoint averaged +13.8 bps — the winners you never got).
- **dn6:** 13.7% filled vs 49.0% missed.
- Even seed-unanimity — the 49.7%-hit subset that made the breakeven arithmetic look reachable — collapses to **27.2% filled vs 80.0% missed** (up6 @ 0.01%).

The economic logic closes: run.007's case for maker entry was "full-maker 4 bp RT drops breakeven hit to ≈49%, and unanimity achieves 49.7%." But posting a limit doesn't sample the trigger distribution — it samples the *pullback-conditional* distribution, where the hit rate is ~13–27%, nowhere near breakeven. The signal fires on momentum onset; a pullback deep enough to fill you is itself evidence the move died.

Both pre-registered rescue routes also failed:

- **wait×δ grid:** every cell negative (sim −13 to −18); deeper δ only lowers fill% without improving filled-hit — the adverse selection gets worse, not better, the more price improvement you demand.
- **h2-timed hybrid** (the explicit run.009 candidate check in the failure guide): up6 −10.37 with 84.5% routed taker (it degenerates to plain taker), dn6 −15.11. Not positive → not a candidate.

## The second promise: entry-delay stress — a genuinely useful null

taker+1bar barely moves anything: up6 −10.04 → −10.36, dn6 −12.93 → −12.76 (CIs overlap almost completely). So run.007's taker numbers were **not** an artifact of the zero-latency entry assumption. The edge is latency-robust — it's simply ~10–13 bps below water on fees, honestly.

Fold 2's regime collapse repeated (filled hit 2.4%/6.2% vs 9–20% elsewhere), same pattern as run.007. Non-overlapping 1-BTC maker trades: −$47k/−$40k, worse than the taker equivalents.

## Where this leaves the project

Three execution levers have now each been tested with pre-registered gates and each returned a clean answer:

1. **Exits** (run.007): TP/SL-at-θ ≈ hold-to-endpoint at short horizons — worth ~nothing.
2. **Entry price** (run.008): maker entry is net-negative via adverse selection — worth less than nothing.
3. **Latency** (run.008): not the problem.

The per-trade loss is therefore not an execution problem — it's a **prediction problem**: 26.5% trigger hit vs ~58.5% taker breakeven.
